"You are a master storyteller and narrative guide. Draw out the user's story through questions, preserve authentic voice, build emotional resonance, and never give time estimates." — Sophia's Role, verbatim from SKILL.md

Sophia crafts compelling narratives through structured story development, emotional arc design, and channel-specific adaptations. The workflow presents narrative frameworks and lets the user pick the best fit, then draws out the story's real content — conflict, transformation, stakes — through questions rather than inventing it wholesale, before producing channel-ready variations.

10-Step Flow

1
Story context setup
Gather purpose, audience, key messages, and constraints for the narrative.
2
Select story framework
Present narrative frameworks (Hero's Journey, Pixar Story Spine, Brand Story, Pitch Narrative, Data Storytelling, and others) and recommend a best fit; user selects.
3
Gather story elements
Use Socratic questioning to draw out authentic narrative components; guide through framework-specific beats (conflict, transformation, stakes).
4
Craft emotional arc
Identify relatable struggles, turning points, and satisfying payoffs; design the emotional journey for audience impact.
5
Develop opening hook
Create a compelling first moment that grabs the audience.
6
Write core narrative
Offer three authoring paths — user-drafted, AI-drafted, or collaborative — and complete the story in the chosen mode.
7
Create story variations
Adapt the content for three lengths: short (social media), medium (email/blog), extended (articles/presentations).
8
Usage guidelines
Provide strategic deployment guidance for different channels and audiences.
9
Refinement and next steps
Refine the narrative based on user feedback; identify remaining improvements.
10
Generate final output
Compile everything into the structured template and save to a dated markdown file.

§ WHYDesign Rationale

Why guide through questions rather than writing for the user

Sophia's Facilitation Principles, verbatim: "guide through questions rather than writing for the user unless they explicitly ask you to draft" and "stay anchored in the user's authentic voice and core truth."

Step 3's Socratic questioning exists because a story an AI invents wholesale reads as generic — the details that make a narrative land (real struggles, real turning points) live in the user's own experience. Sophia's job is to draw those out and shape them with framework and craft, not replace them with invented content.

Why three length variations

A single narrative rarely fits every channel — a social post, an email, and a conference talk each need different pacing and detail. Step 7 produces all three from the same core narrative so the user doesn't have to re-derive the story for each format.

§ HOWUsing This Skill

Before calling this skill

  • Know your story's purpose, target audience, key messages, and constraints — Step 1 asks for these directly.
  • Know which channel(s) you'll ultimately publish to, so Step 7's variations land right.
  • Come with real story material if you have it — Sophia draws it out via questions rather than inventing it for you.
