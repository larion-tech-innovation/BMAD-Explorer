"You are a strategic innovation advisor. Demand brutal truth about market realities, challenge assumptions ruthlessly, balance bold vision with pragmatic execution, and never give time estimates." — Victor's Role, verbatim from SKILL.md

Victor identifies disruption opportunities and architects business model innovation through rigorous market analysis, option development, and execution planning. The workflow moves in one direction only — outward first, then narrow: understand the market and the current model in brutal detail, hunt disruption vectors across four established frameworks, generate a wide set of innovation options, then converge on one bold recommendation backed by a phased execution roadmap and a real risk-mitigation plan.

9-Step Flow

1
Establish strategic context
Gather company background, strategic drivers, current business model, constraints, and success definition. Synthesize into a clear strategic framing.
2
Analyze market landscape and competitive dynamics
Market analysis using 2-4 frameworks: TAM/SAM/SOM, Five Forces, competitive positioning, market timing assessment.
3
Analyze current business model⚡ checkpoint
Deconstruct the existing model via Business Model Canvas and Value Proposition Canvas, plus revenue/cost structure analysis. Identify vulnerabilities and competitive advantages.
4
Identify disruption opportunities
Hunt for disruption vectors using 2-3 frameworks: Disruptive Innovation Theory, Jobs to be Done, Blue Ocean Strategy, Platform Revolution — focused on non-consumers, underserved jobs, technology enablers.
5
Generate innovation opportunities⚡ checkpoint
Develop 5-10 concrete innovation options across business model, value chain, partnerships, and technology dimensions using Three Horizons plus 2-4 additional frameworks.
6
Develop and evaluate strategic options
Synthesize insights into 3 distinct strategic options with detailed analysis: description, implications, resource requirements, risks, expected outcomes.
7
Recommend strategic direction
Make a bold strategic recommendation with rationale, critical hypotheses, and success factors.
8
Build execution roadmap⚡ checkpoint
Phased roadmap across three phases — Immediate Impact, Foundation Building, Scale & Optimization — with initiatives, resources, metrics, decision gates.
9
Define metrics and risk mitigation
Establish a measurement framework — leading/lagging indicators, decision gates, comprehensive risk mitigation strategies.

§ WHYDesign Rationale

Why brutal truth over comfort

Victor's Facilitation Principles, verbatim: "demand brutal truth about market realities before innovation exploration" and "challenge assumptions ruthlessly; comfortable illusions kill strategies."

Innovation strategy fails most often not from a lack of ideas but from decision-makers protecting a comfortable read of their current market position. Steps 2-3 force a rigorous, framework-driven audit of the market and the existing business model before any disruption-hunting begins, so the options generated in Steps 4-6 are grounded in what's actually true, not what's comfortable to believe.

Why energy checkpoints at steps 3, 5, 8

This workflow is analytically dense — three separate framework-driven deep dives (current-model teardown, disruption-vector hunting, roadmap-building) each ask for sustained strategic thinking. Rather than pushing straight through nine analytically heavy steps, the SKILL.md pauses for a real check-in, verbatim:

"We've covered market landscape. How's your energy? This next part — deconstructing your business model — requires honest self-assessment. Ready?" (after Step 2, before Step 3)
"We've got the strategy direction. How's your energy for the execution planning — turning strategy into actionable roadmap?" (after Step 7, before Step 8)
