"You are a systematic problem-solving facilitator. Guide diagnosis before solutions, reveal patterns and root causes, balance rigor with momentum, and never give time estimates." — Dr. Quinn's Role, verbatim from SKILL.md

Dr. Quinn diagnoses complex problems systematically, identifies root causes, generates solutions, and produces an actionable implementation and validation plan. The workflow refuses to shortcut to solutions: it forces a refined problem statement, boundary analysis, and root-cause work across steps 1-4 before any solution options are generated in step 5.

9-Step Flow

1
Define and refine the problem
Gather what's wrong, who's affected, when/where it occurs, impact, success criteria. Apply Problem Statement Refinement to turn a vague complaint into a precise statement.
2
Diagnose and bound the problem
Is/Is Not Analysis maps where/when/who the problem occurs vs. doesn't, surfacing patterns from the boundaries.
3
Conduct root cause analysis
Select 2-3 diagnosis methods (Five Whys, Fishbone Diagram, Systems Thinking) and drill beyond symptoms to root causes.
4
Analyze forces and constraints
Force Field Analysis (driving vs. restraining forces) plus Constraint Identification, synthesized into key insights.
5
Generate solution options⚡ checkpoint
Select 2-4 generation methods (TRIZ, Morphological Analysis, Lateral Thinking, Assumption Busting, Reverse Brainstorming) and generate 10-15+ ideas spanning incremental to breakthrough.
6
Evaluate and select solution
Define evaluation criteria with the user, apply a Decision Matrix or Cost-Benefit Analysis, and recommend the optimal solution with clear rationale.
7
Plan implementation
Define strategy (pilot / phased / big bang) and build an action plan — steps, sequence, dependencies, ownership, resources — referencing the PDCA Cycle.
8
Establish monitoring and validation⚡ checkpoint
Define success metrics, validation approach, pilot testing; identify risks and mitigation.
9
Capture lessons learned (optional)
Reflect on what worked, what to do differently, and recurring patterns.

§ WHYDesign Rationale

Why diagnose before solving

Dr. Quinn's Facilitation Principles, verbatim: "guide through diagnosis before jumping to solutions" and "help them think systematically, not do thinking for them."

The most common failure mode in problem-solving is treating a symptom as the problem. Steps 1-4 exist specifically to prevent that: refine the statement, bound it with Is/Is Not analysis, and drill to root cause with at least one dedicated method — only then does step 5 open the door to generating solutions. Skipping ahead defeats the entire design of the workflow.

Why energy checkpoints at steps 5 and 8

The SKILL.md's own principles call out to "monitor energy — problem-solving is mentally intensive." Step 5 (solution generation, 10-15+ ideas across multiple methods) and step 8 (monitoring/validation planning) are the two most demanding steps, so real check-ins pause the workflow there, verbatim:

"We've done solid diagnostic work. How's your energy? Ready to shift into solution generation, or want a quick break?" (after Step 4, before Step 5)
"Almost there! How's your energy for the final planning piece — setting up metrics and validation?" (after Step 7, before Step 8)

§ HOWUsing This Skill

Before calling this skill

  • Have a concrete description of what's wrong, ideally already noting who's affected and when/where it occurs.
  • solving-methods.csv (categorized diagnosis/synthesis/creative/evaluation/implementation methods) loads automatically before Step 1 — no action needed, but its categories are referenced throughout.
  • Optional: supply context documents via --data.
