II · Workflow Skills

The four real step-by-step processes, plus the cross-listed core skill. Click "what · why · how" to open the full guide in a new tab. Every workflow skill checkpoints after each saved section: [a] Advanced Elicitation · [c] Continue · [p] Party-Mode · [y] YOLO.

Innovation Strategy
Victor · bmad-cis-innovation-strategy
Identify disruption opportunities and architect business model innovation through rigorous market analysis, option development, and execution planning. Never gives time estimates.
  1. Establish strategic context — company background, drivers, model, constraints, success definition
  2. Analyze market landscape & competitive dynamics — TAM/SAM/SOM, Five Forces, positioning
  3. Analyze current business model — Business Model Canvas, Value Proposition Canvas⚡ checkpoint
  4. Identify disruption opportunities — Disruptive Innovation Theory, Jobs to be Done, Blue Ocean
  5. Generate innovation opportunities — 5-10 options via Three Horizons + 2-4 frameworks⚡ checkpoint
  6. Develop & evaluate strategic options — 3 distinct options, fully analyzed
  7. Recommend strategic direction — bold recommendation, rationale, hypotheses
  8. Build execution roadmap — Immediate Impact / Foundation / Scale phases⚡ checkpoint
  9. Define metrics & risk mitigation — leading/lagging indicators, decision gates
OUT: {output_folder}/innovation-strategy-{date}.md
Problem Solving
Dr. Quinn · bmad-cis-problem-solving
Diagnose complex problems systematically, identify root causes, generate solutions, and produce an actionable implementation and validation plan.
  1. Define & refine the problem — what's wrong, who's affected, impact, success criteria
  2. Diagnose & bound the problem — Is/Is Not Analysis
  3. Conduct root cause analysis — Five Whys, Fishbone, Systems Thinking
  4. Analyze forces & constraints — Force Field Analysis
  5. Generate solution options — 10-15+ ideas via TRIZ, Lateral Thinking, more⚡ checkpoint
  6. Evaluate & select solution — Decision Matrix or Cost-Benefit Analysis
  7. Plan implementation — pilot / phased / big bang, PDCA Cycle
  8. Establish monitoring & validation — success metrics, risk mitigation⚡ checkpoint
  9. Capture lessons learned (optional) — what worked, patterns
OUT: {output_folder}/problem-solution-{date}.md
Design Thinking
Maya · bmad-cis-design-thinking
Guide human-centered design through empathy, definition, ideation, prototyping, and testing. Keeps users at the center of every decision.
  1. Gather context & define design challenge
  2. EMPATHIZE — 3-5 user research methods, empathy map
  3. DEFINE — POV statements, "How Might We" questions⚡ checkpoint
  4. IDEATE — 15-30 ideas, defer judgment, cluster
  5. PROTOTYPE — low-fidelity, minimum testable features⚡ checkpoint
  6. TEST — validate with 5-7 real users
  7. Plan next iteration — refinements, action items⚡ checkpoint
OUT: {output_folder}/design-thinking-{date}.md
Storytelling
Sophia · bmad-cis-storytelling
Craft compelling narratives through structured story development, emotional arc design, and channel-specific adaptations. Guides through questions, never writes for you unasked.
  1. Story context setup — purpose, audience, key messages
  2. Select story framework — Hero's Journey, Pixar Story Spine, and more
  3. Gather story elements — Socratic questioning
  4. Craft emotional arc — struggles, turning points, payoffs
  5. Develop opening hook
  6. Write core narrative — user-drafted, AI-drafted, or collaborative
  7. Create story variations — short / medium / extended
  8. Usage guidelines — deployment guidance by channel
  9. Refinement & next steps
  10. Generate final output
OUT: {output_folder}/story-{date}.md

III · CIS-Specific Concepts

Diverge-Before-Converge

Every workflow explicitly expands possibilities before narrowing — the defining structural principle of CIS, contrasting with BMM's linear convergence.

Energy Checkpoints

Real in-workflow check-ins (e.g. Victor: "How's your energy? This next part — deconstructing your business model — requires honest self-assessment.") at Innovation Strategy steps 3/5/8, Problem Solving steps 5/8, and Design Thinking steps 3/5/7.

Template-Output Checkpoint

After every saved section: [a] Advanced Elicitation, [c] Continue, [p] Party-Mode, [y] YOLO — verbatim from every workflow's Behavioral Constraints.

Layered Customization

customize.toml base → team (_bmad/custom/{skill}.toml) → personal ({skill}.user.toml), merged via resolve_customization.py. Introduced v0.2.0.

visual_tools Config

Unique CIS config key (_bmad/cis/config.yaml) — Mermaid, Excalidraw, Gemini Nano, or Other/Manual. Not present in BMM.

Not Installed by Default

CIS requires an explicit module install — it is not part of a standard new BMAD installation.
