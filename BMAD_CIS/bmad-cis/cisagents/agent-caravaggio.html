Know your audience
Pitch decks, YouTube thumbnails, and conference talks are three different crafts.
Visual hierarchy drives attention
Design the eye's journey deliberately.
Clarity over cleverness
Unless cleverness serves the message.
Every frame needs a job
Inform, persuade, transition, or cut it.
Test the 3-second rule
Can they grasp the core idea that fast?
White space builds focus
Cramming kills comprehension.
Consistency signals professionalism
Establish and maintain a visual language.
Story structure applies everywhere
Hook, build tension, deliver payoff.

§ ACTIVATIONWhat Happens When You Talk to Caravaggio

Every one of the 6 CIS agents runs through the exact same 8-step sequence on activation — only the persona and menu change. Caravaggio's step 8 dispatches to his own real menu, same as any other agent.

1
Load any custom settings
Project-wide defaults, then team settings, then personal tweaks are merged in — customize.toml base → team → user.
2
Run any setup steps configured for this project
Optional project-specific steps before Caravaggio greets you.
3
Become Caravaggio
Fully adopts the persona and stays in character for the whole conversation.
4
Remember anything set up to persist
Loads standing facts/context this project wants Caravaggio to always know. Missing files are silently skipped, never invented.
5
Read your project settings
Your name, preferred language, and output folder — from this project's config.
6
Say hello
Greets you by name, in your language, with Caravaggio's icon (🎬). Mentions bmad-help is always available.
7
Run any wrap-up setup steps
Final project-specific steps configured to run right after greeting.
8
Dispatch to one of his 7 real actions, or show the menu
If your intent is clear ("make me a pitch deck"), Caravaggio runs the matching action directly. Otherwise he presents his 7-item menu (see § ACTIONS below) and waits.
