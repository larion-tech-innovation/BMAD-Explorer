The Research Trio covers three orthogonal failure modes. Market Research validates who the customers are and what the competitive landscape looks like. Domain Research maps the industry's vocabulary, regulations, and expert mental models. Technical Research evaluates technology feasibility, stack alternatives, and architecture patterns. Each skill is independent — run one, two, or all three depending on what the team doesn't yet know.

order Recommended Execution Order

When running more than one skill, the order matters — each skill's output informs the next.

1
Market Research · first
Understand the commercial landscape
Run first to confirm the segment exists, understand who the customers are, and see what competitors already offer. The findings define scope for Domain Research — you know which regulations and vocabulary are relevant because you know which market you're entering.
2
Domain Research · second
Map the industry's expert mental models
Run second to learn the regulations, vocabulary, and value chain of the domain the market research identified. The regulatory findings directly affect which technology constraints Technical Research needs to evaluate.
3
Technical Research · third
Confirm feasibility against real constraints
Run last — after you know what market you're building for and what regulations apply. Technical Research then evaluates architecture options against those real constraints rather than in the abstract.

§ WHY Why Research Before PRD?

Writing a PRD before researching is writing fiction. Each research track removes a specific class of blind spots that are expensive to discover in Phase 4 or after launch.

blind-spots Three Blind Spots — One Per Track

Dimension skipped Blind spot Typical failure
Skip Market Research Wrong customer assumptions, no competitor visibility, pricing in the dark Pricing users won't accept; discovering a competitor had the same feature two years ago; building for a persona that does not exist in the actual customer base
Skip Domain Research Wrong vocabulary, missing regulations, expert mental models unknown Healthcare app using marketer language that doctors reject; HIPAA violation discovered at launch; product design that conflicts with a fundamental industry workflow
Skip Technical Research Wrong feasibility estimate, vendor lock-in, missed stack alternatives Phase 4 discovering the chosen database doesn't meet compliance requirements; epic rewrite because the architecture doesn't scale; integration that took 2 weeks estimated takes 6 months

Compounding effect: All three blind spots compound. A team that doesn't know the market can't write a useful domain glossary. A team that doesn't know the domain can't write accurate regulatory constraints for the architect. An architect without constraints designs for the wrong system. Research Trio breaks this compounding cycle before it starts.

§ HOW Before Calling the Skills

Everything to have ready before invoking any Research Trio skill. Gathering this upfront produces richer, more targeted output — the agent can only surface constraints it knows about.

Universal prerequisite: Web search must be active in your Claude Code session. All three research skills require live web search — they do not fall back to training data alone. The agent will confirm search availability in Step 1 of each skill.

Skills That Help Market Research Go Smoothly

  • JTBD thinkingUnderstanding why a customer "hires" a product — not demographics but jobs. Helps you ask sharper research questions and interpret the output more precisely.
  • Pain vs. symptom"Customers complain about slow loading" = symptom. "Can't close deals without live data" = pain point. Knowing the difference helps you frame the research question correctly.
  • Competitive intelFamiliarity with App Store / G2 reviews, LinkedIn job postings, annual reports. These are the sources the agent draws from most — knowing how to read them speeds interpretation.

Four simultaneous searches on what the customer struggles with. Primary source: G2 / Capterra / App Store reviews, Reddit threads, community discussions.

Four simultaneous searches on how customers evaluate and choose solutions. Sources: analyst reports, vendor case studies, B2B buying research.

Deep dive on competitors — both known players and newly discovered ones. Sources: SEC filings, earnings calls, product websites, G2 comparisons.

Synthesizes all prior steps into a structured document with executive summary, customer profiles, competitive map, and strategic recommendations. User selects [C] to save.

Skills That Help Domain Research Go Smoothly

  • Industry classificationKnowing whether the domain is regulated vs. high-growth tech vs. emerging helps calibrate depth expectations before starting.
  • Value chain awarenessUnderstanding that regulations often sit at specific handoff points in the value chain speeds up Step 4 (Regulatory Focus).
  • Regulatory literacyKnowing the difference between a regulation (legal requirement) and a standard (voluntary best practice) prevents misclassifying findings in the output.

bmad-technical-research Technical Research — What to Prepare

Preparation Checklist bmad-technical-research · before session
ItemWhat's neededPriority
Technology questionSpecific: "Compare GraphQL vs REST for our use case" — not "research about APIs." The more precise, the more actionable the output.Required
Architecture contextThe Architecture Document from Phase 3, or a description of the current/planned architecture.Required
ConstraintsLanguage/framework constraints, team expertise limits, licensing requirements, compliance constraints from Domain Research.Required
Comparison candidatesList specific technologies to compare — the agent will parallel-analyze each one explicitly.Required
Decision deadlineTechnical Research feeds into Solution Design — knowing the deadline helps the agent calibrate depth vs. speed.Helpful

Skills That Help Technical Research Go Smoothly

  • Architecture literacyKnowing the difference between monolith, microservices, and serverless patterns means the agent can skip introductory framing and go straight to your specific trade-off question.
  • Integration thinkingUnderstanding that most systems integrate with existing services helps define the constraint space before the session starts.
  • Performance baselineHaving rough performance requirements (e.g. "p99 < 200ms", "10k concurrent users") lets the agent evaluate scalability patterns against real targets.

Technical Research — 6-Step Process

1
Technical Scope Confirmation
Confirm 5 areas: Architecture, Implementation, Stack, Integration, Performance
No web search yet. Agent defines the research scope across 5 technical dimensions. User approves before research begins.
2
Technology Stack Analysis · 4 parallel searches
Languages, frameworks, databases, cloud services
Four simultaneous searches comparing technologies on the candidate list. Sources: official docs, Stack Overflow surveys, GitHub activity, Hacker News discussions.
3
Integration Patterns · 4 parallel searches
API patterns, protocols, microservices, event-driven
Four simultaneous searches on integration and communication patterns relevant to the architecture context.
4
Architectural Patterns · 3 searches
Architecture patterns, design principles, scalability
Three searches targeting known architectural patterns and their trade-offs at the scale and context described in the architecture document.
5
Implementation Research · 3 searches
Adoption strategies, DevOps practices, operational considerations
Three searches on how teams implement and operate the technologies in practice. Sources: engineering blogs, conference talks, case studies.
6
Research Synthesis
12-section document with implementation roadmap and risk assessment
Synthesizes all prior steps into a structured technical reference covering stack analysis, architecture patterns, integration, security, and strategic recommendations. User selects [C] to save.

shared-mechanics Shared Mechanics — All Three Skills

All Research Trio skills share the same execution model. Understanding these mechanics helps you predict the session flow for any of the three skills.

Execution Mechanics Research Trio · all 3 skills
MechanicHow it works
Step gatingUser must select [C] Continue to advance each step. The agent never proceeds automatically. This lets you review findings before the next search layer begins.
Document continuityOutput file is created from research.template.md at Step 1. Each step appends its findings to the document — content is never held in memory and written at the end.
Frontmatter trackingstepsCompleted: [] in the output file's YAML frontmatter is updated after each step. On re-activation, the agent reads this value and offers to resume from the last completed step.
Parallel web searchSteps 2–5 in all three skills execute multiple web searches simultaneously. Market and Domain Research run 4 parallel searches per step; Technical Research runs 3–4. This is the main speed driver.
Immediate writeContent is written to the document as it is generated — not buffered until [C]. If the session is interrupted, findings from completed steps are already saved.
Source verificationEvery factual claim carries a URL citation. When two sources conflict, the conflict is presented explicitly rather than resolved silently — the user decides which to trust.
Output path_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/research/{type}-{topic-slug}-research-{date}.md where {type} is market, domain, or technical.
