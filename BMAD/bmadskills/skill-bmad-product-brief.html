§ WHAT Product Brief — What does this Skill do?

Skill code
bmad-product-brief
Menu code
CB (invoked via /bmad-agent-analyst → CB)
Role
Strategic document facilitator — produces a 1–2 page brief for go/no-go decisions.
Flags
--headless / -H — skip interactive coaching, auto-produce brief from available inputs; halt with blocked if intent is ambiguous.
Continuity
If a draft from a prior session exists → offer resume before starting fresh.

This skill turns a conversation (or existing source material) into a concise, decision-ready Product Brief. It facilitates discovery, infers answers from any source artifacts provided, calibrates rigor to the stakes of the effort, and writes a brief that a stakeholder can read in 5 minutes and make a go/no-go call from. All decisions, changes, and overrides are written to a real-time decision log that serves as institutional memory.

Before PRD hand-off or stakeholder review

output-structure Output Structure

Three files are created for every brief. The workspace is created at intent confirmation — not at the end of the session.

_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/briefs/brief-{project}-{date}/
├── brief.md           ← primary brief document (1-2 pages)
├── addendum.md        ← depth for downstream (personas, market data,
│                         rejected rationale, parked roadmap)
└── .decision-log.md   ← canonical memory: every decision, change,
                          override recorded real-time
  • brief.mdPrimary stakeholder artifact. Strictly 1–2 pages. Executive-ready. Written for go/no-go decisions, not engineering intake.
  • addendum.mdOverflow and depth document for downstream teams. Holds detailed personas, market data, rejected rationale, and parked roadmap items that would bloat the brief.
  • .decision-log.mdCanonical institutional memory. Every choice, pivot, override, and assumption is timestamped and recorded here in real-time. Prefixed with . to signal it is a meta-artifact, not a deliverable.

template 8-Section Brief Template canonical

Every brief produced by this skill follows the same 8-section structure. Content that would push the brief beyond 2 pages is moved to addendum.md.

8-Section Template Target length: 1–2 pages
SectionContents
Executive Summary What this is · what problem it solves · why it matters · why now. 2–3 paragraphs. Must stand alone — a reader who reads only this section should understand the case for building.
The Problem Who feels the pain · how they cope today · cost of status quo. Real scenarios, not abstractions. Written so the reader feels the problem, not just understands it.
The Solution Experience and outcome, not implementation. How it solves the problem described above. No technology choices. No architecture. Pure value.
What Makes This Different Key differentiators and unfair advantage. Honest — no fabricated moats. If differentiation is thin, say so and suggest where to develop it.
Who This Serves Primary users (vivid but brief) · secondary users if relevant · what success looks like for each. Detailed personas → addendum.
Success Criteria User success signals + business objectives. Measurable. Avoids vanity metrics. Grounded in the problem statement above.
Scope What is in for v1 · what is explicitly out. A boundary document, not a feature list. Out-of-scope items move to addendum roadmap if they have future potential.
Vision Where this goes in 2–3 years if it succeeds. Inspiring but grounded. Should feel like a natural extension of the problem and solution, not a separate ambition.

§ WHY Why a Product Brief? — The reasoning behind each design choice

why-brief-before-prd Why a Brief Before a PRD?

A PRD covers requirements — what must be built and how it should behave. A brief covers why to build it at all. These are different audiences, different decisions, and different timeframes. The brief is written for executives and stakeholders making a go/no-go call. The PRD is written for the product and engineering teams implementing the decision. Conflating the two produces documents that satisfy neither audience.

why-one-two-pages Why 1–2 Pages?

Stakeholders make go/no-go decisions in 5 minutes — a long document is a signal that the concept is not clear yet. If you cannot explain the business case in two pages, the concept needs more thinking, not more writing. The 1–2 page constraint forces clarity. Longer supporting content lives in addendum.md for teams that need the depth.

why-decision-log Why a Decision Log?

After 6 months, no one remembers why the scope was scoped the way it was, why a differentiator was called out or removed, or why the vision statement changed. The decision log is the institutional memory of the brief's evolution. It records every choice, change, override, and assumption in real-time — not as a retrospective. When the brief is later updated or validated, the log is the first thing read.

why-three-intents Why 3 Intents?

The same underlying skill serves the full lifecycle of a product concept. Create is for when the concept is new. Update is for when the concept has evolved, pivoted, or received new signal. Validate is for when the brief needs an honest critique before it moves downstream to PRD or stakeholder review. Using the same skill across all three ensures the output format and decision log are consistent throughout the concept's life.

skills 3 · Skills That Help This Session

Facilitator Mindset bmad-product-brief · session quality
SkillWhy it matters
Concise description The agent opens with "tell me everything" — but a 1–2 sentence seed is more useful than a 10-paragraph dump. Lead with the core concept, then add detail when probed.
Stakeholder fluency Know who requested this feature and why — the agent uses this to calibrate rigor and positioning language in the brief.
Stakes awareness Know in advance whether this brief is for internal use, an investor pitch, or a public launch. Stakes determine how hard the agent pushes on market positioning and differentiator claims.
Decision readiness The session flows fastest when you know: Fast Path (draft quickly, react) or Coaching Path (walk together)? Choose based on whether the concept is clear or still forming.

per-step 4 · Per-Step Preparation

Within the session, each phase has a readiness moment. Having these answers ready prevents pauses that break flow.

Phase-Level Readiness bmad-product-brief · create intent
PhaseWhat to have ready
Brain Dump Everything you know: feature concept, source material, research notes, prior briefs, stakeholder emails. Don't edit yourself — the agent filters, infers, and asks about gaps.
Infer Phase Gather all related artifacts before the session — brainstorming output, PRFAQ if run, design discussions. The more you provide, the fewer clarifying questions the agent needs to ask.
Stakes Read Decide in advance: passion project · internal pitch · investor input · public launch. This calibrates rigor on market positioning, success criteria, and differentiation claims.
Working Mode Fast Path if you have a well-defined concept and time is the constraint. Coaching Path if the concept is still forming or you've never authored a brief before.
Discovery (Coaching Path) Prepare answers for three areas: (1) Problem and context — what, why now, who asked; (2) Users — who specifically, current workflow, what success looks like; (3) Solution shape — high-level concept is sufficient at this stage.

Finalize — 4-Step Protocol
StepWhat Happens
Decision Log Audit Walk .decision-log.md entry by entry. Confirm each decision is captured in the brief or addendum, or explicitly set aside with a reason. The log is the institutional memory — do not skip.
Polish Two parallel editorial passes: bmad-editorial-review-structure (section order, internal consistency, logical flow) + bmad-editorial-review-prose (tone, clarity, readability). Brief first; addendum after.
External Handoffs Route brief to configured external destinations (Confluence, Notion, or other configured tools). If a destination is unavailable or unconfigured, skip and flag — do not block finalization.
Suggest Next Steps Invoke bmad-help to suggest the next skill. Typical next: if go/no-go is affirmative → bmad-create-prd or bmad-prfaq. If concept still developing → bmad-market-research or bmad-domain-research.
